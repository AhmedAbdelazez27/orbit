About SpaceWatch & Documentation

Understanding the technology and purpose behind SpaceWatch.

Project Purpose

SpaceWatch is a high-fidelity orbital tracking and collision avoidance system designed for satellite operators, researchers, and space agencies. Our mission is to enhance space situational awareness (SSA) by providing accurate, real-time predictions of satellite and debris trajectories, thereby mitigating collision risks and promoting safer space operations.

We utilize the standard SGP4 propagation model, significantly enhanced with proprietary machine learning algorithms trained on historical tracking data. This hybrid approach allows for improved accuracy, especially for objects in complex orbital regimes or during periods of high solar activity.

Model Performance Across Time Horizons

Short-Term Accuracy (0-24 Hours)

For immediate conjunction analysis and maneuver planning, the ML model provides a critical accuracy boost over the standard SGP4, ensuring safer short-term operations.

Medium-Term Accuracy (1-7 Days)

In medium-term forecasts, SGP4's errors begin to compound. Our ML model's advantage grows, delivering significantly greater accuracy for weekly operational planning.

Long-Term Accuracy (>7 Days)

The ML-enhanced model is transformative for long-range ephemeris, reducing the Mean Absolute Error (MAE) by over 98% and enabling unprecedented catalog maintenance.

Documentation

SGP4 Propagation Model

The Simplified General Perturbations 4 (SGP4) model is a standard analytical method used to predict the orbits of near-Earth objects. It accounts for secular and periodic variations caused by Earth's oblateness and atmospheric drag.

References: Hoots, Felix R., and Ronald L. Roehrich. "Models for Propagation of NORAD Element Sets." Spacetrack Report No. 3, USAF Project SPACE TRACK, December 1980.

Machine Learning Corrections

Our ML model analyzes discrepancies between SGP4 predictions and observed data (e.g., radar measurements, laser ranging) to learn systematic error patterns. It then applies corrections to future SGP4 outputs, improving prediction accuracy.

The model architecture (e.g., LSTM, Transformer) and training data specifics are proprietary but are continuously updated based on performance monitoring.

Data Sources

SpaceWatch aggregates data from multiple public and private sources, including:

  • Space-Track.org (Official USSPACECOM catalog)
  • Celestrak (Dr. T.S. Kelso's curated data)
  • Commercial SSA providers (e.g., LeoLabs)
  • Proprietary sensor networks (where applicable)

Data is ingested, validated, and cross-referenced before being used in our models.

Collision Risk Assessment

Collision probability is calculated using covariance data associated with object states. We employ methods like the Foster-Akella algorithm or Monte Carlo simulations for high-risk conjunctions.

Alert levels (Low, Medium, High, Critical) are based on probability thresholds, miss distance, and object types.
